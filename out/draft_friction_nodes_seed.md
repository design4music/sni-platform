# Friction Nodes — Seed List Draft (v0)

**Date**: 2026-05-06
**Status**: Working draft. 12 seed FNs to stress-test the v2 narrative worksheet.
**Companion**: `out/concept_friction_nodes_and_narratives_v2.md`, `out/narrative_consolidation_pass1_v2.md`.

**Format per FN**:
- `id`, `name`, `description`
- `centroid_ids[]` — where the phenomenon manifests
- `topic_keywords[]` — neutral routing terms (event filter)
- `competing_narratives[]` — references to v2 worksheet narratives (N1-N31). Missing narratives flagged in closing section, never invented as if they exist.
- `competing_frames` — how each narrative reads this specific phenomenon

Selection criteria applied: ≥2 narratives with mutually incompatible prescriptions; persistent (5-year horizon, not transient flashpoint); structurally diverse (territorial / technological / normative-regime / institutional / chokepoint); biased toward FNs the April "5 cross-country zones" model would have missed.

## FN1. `taiwan_status`

**Name**: Taiwan status

**Description**: A 23-million-person de facto state with its own military, currency, and democracy that the PRC claims as a province and that no major power formally recognises but most arm and protect indirectly. The contested phenomenon is not "will China invade" but the entire ambiguous status: whose law applies in the strait, whose chips Taiwan exports to, who transits which waters, whose troops train there. Every PLA exercise, every transit, every chip-export-control update is a move in this contest. Endures regardless of whether the 2026 news cycle is hot or cool.

**centroid_ids**: `[ASIA-CHINA, ASIA-TAIWAN, AMERICAS-USA, ASIA-JAPAN, OCEANIA-AUSTRALIA]`

**topic_keywords**: `Taiwan`, `Taiwan Strait`, `PLA exercise`, `median line`, `TSMC`, `cross-strait`, `Tsai`, `Lai`, `One China`, `arms sale Taiwan`, `transit Taiwan Strait`

**competing_narratives**: `[N2 west_china_strategic_competition, N5 multipolar_anti_us_hegemony, N28 china_pla_modernisation_defensive]`

**competing_frames**:
- N2: PLA activity around Taiwan is grey-zone coercion and rehearsal for invasion; semiconductor dependence is a strategic vulnerability requiring AUKUS, Quad, and forward presence in the first island chain.
- N5: US arms sales and transits are the actual provocation; Washington manufactures a Taiwan crisis to legitimise containment of China and sustain hegemonic alliance architecture.
- N28: PLA modernisation and Taiwan reunification are restoration of sovereignty and proportionate defence under US containment — not aggression but inevitability.

## FN2. `iran_nuclear_program`

**Name**: Iran nuclear program

**Description**: Iran's enrichment, breakout time, and centrifuge cascade are simultaneously read as proximate to a weapon (existential threat read) and as a sovereign civilian-energy and deterrence-hedge program (resistance read). The contested phenomenon is the same set of technical facts: stockpile mass, enrichment percentage, IAEA access, advanced-centrifuge counts. Each frame produces incompatible prescriptions — preemptive strike vs sanctions relief vs strategic ambiguity. Persistent: even a deal would be re-contested.

**centroid_ids**: `[MIDEAST-IRAN, MIDEAST-ISRAEL, AMERICAS-USA, MIDEAST-SAUDI, NON-STATE-EU]`

**topic_keywords**: `Iran nuclear`, `Natanz`, `Fordow`, `enrichment`, `IAEA Iran`, `JCPOA`, `breakout time`, `centrifuge`, `Arak`, `weapons-grade`

**competing_narratives**: `[N3 west_iran_proxy_threat, N5 multipolar_anti_us_hegemony]`

**competing_frames**:
- N3 (nuclear slice specifically — see audit Section A on splitting N3): enrichment levels are an existential threat warranting preemptive strike; maximum-pressure sanctions and military action to deny breakout capability.
- N5: enrichment is a sovereign right of a state under sanctions and threat; US-Israeli pressure is hegemonic interference and the actual escalator.

*Note*: this FN is the strongest argument for splitting N3 into a nuclear half and a proxy-network half (Output 2 Section A). Currently a single v2 narrative covers both Natanz and Hezbollah-tunnels with one keyword bag, which is exactly the split symptom.

## FN3. `ukraine_territory`

**Name**: Ukraine territorial integrity

**Description**: The post-2022 line of contact and the four annexed oblasts (plus Crimea) are the contested phenomenon: whose sovereignty, whose elections, whose currency, whose troops. Distinct from the broader Russia-NATO contest because the dispute is about *this specific land*. Every weapons-package decision, every negotiation framework, every sanctions adjustment is a move on this FN. Persistent regardless of whether war is hot, frozen, or in ceasefire.

**centroid_ids**: `[EUROPE-UKRAINE, EUROPE-RUSSIA, AMERICAS-USA, NON-STATE-EU, NON-STATE-NATO]`

**topic_keywords**: `Ukraine`, `Donbas`, `Crimea`, `Zaporizhzhia`, `Kherson`, `line of contact`, `territorial integrity Ukraine`, `annexation`, `ceasefire Ukraine`, `peace plan Ukraine`

**competing_narratives**: `[N1 west_russia_threat_response, N4 russia_defensive_imperialism, N9 ukraine_resistance_to_russia, N5 multipolar_anti_us_hegemony]`

**competing_frames**:
- N1: territory is a sovereignty principle; any compromise rewards aggression and emboldens further revisionism. Sustained weapons aid + sanctions until Ukraine restores 1991 borders.
- N4: Donbas/Crimea/Russian-speaking territories are protected populations within the near abroad; the SMO's territorial outcome is corrective, not aggressive.
- N9: every kilometre is paid for in Ukrainian blood; territorial concessions are existential, not negotiable; Western hesitation costs land.
- N5: territorial outcome is a function of multipolar rebalance; Western insistence on 1991 borders is hegemonic posturing that prolongs war.

## FN4. `arctic_governance`

**Name**: Arctic governance and access

**Description**: A region transitioning from frozen-and-quiet to navigable-and-resource-rich. The contested phenomenon is who governs what — the Northwest Passage as Canadian internal waters or international strait, the Northern Sea Route as Russian or international, Greenland's status, Svalbard's treaty regime, Arctic Council membership and authority, near-Arctic state claims. Four narratives converge here and none are satisfied by the others. Persistent on a multi-decade horizon as ice retreats.

**centroid_ids**: `[EUROPE-NORDIC, AMERICAS-CANADA, EUROPE-RUSSIA, ASIA-CHINA, AMERICAS-USA]`

**topic_keywords**: `Arctic`, `Greenland`, `Svalbard`, `Northwest Passage`, `Northern Sea Route`, `Arctic Council`, `polar Silk Road`, `NORAD`, `ice melt`, `near-Arctic`

**competing_narratives**: `[N14 arctic_coastal_sovereignty, N4 russia_defensive_imperialism, N6 china_alternative_order, N7 us_hemispheric_primacy]`

**competing_frames**:
- N14: Arctic is governed by its eight coastal states; Russian militarisation and Chinese near-Arctic claims are encroachment; Northwest Passage is Canadian internal waters.
- N4: Northern Sea Route and Arctic basing are Russian sovereign territory and a defensive response to NATO encroachment from the north flank.
- N6: development-first cooperation, polar Silk Road as legitimate connectivity; bloc-mentality militarisation by NATO is the destabiliser.
- N7: Greenland and Arctic approaches are within the US sphere of strategic primacy; acquisition discourse is hemispheric-security logic.

## FN5. `ai_and_semiconductor_sovereignty`

**Name**: AI capability and semiconductor supply

**Description**: Frontier AI compute and the chips that produce it are the contested phenomenon. Export-control regimes (US Entity List, EUV restrictions, advanced-node licensing), foundry concentration in Taiwan and Korea, indigenous Chinese fab buildout, sovereign-AI-stack programs in the EU and India. Distinct from FN1 (Taiwan status) because the contest is over *capability access and rules*, not *territory*. Persistent over decades — chip generations turn every 18 months but the regime contest does not. The April "active conflict zone" model would not surface this FN at all.

**centroid_ids**: `[AMERICAS-USA, ASIA-CHINA, ASIA-TAIWAN, ASIA-SOUTHKOREA, NON-STATE-EU, ASIA-JAPAN, ASIA-INDIA]`

**topic_keywords**: `semiconductor`, `chip`, `EUV`, `ASML`, `TSMC`, `SMIC`, `Entity List`, `export control`, `AI chip`, `H100`, `Nvidia China`, `indigenous fab`, `CHIPS Act`, `sovereign AI`, `Huawei Ascend`

**competing_narratives**: `[N2 west_china_strategic_competition, N5 multipolar_anti_us_hegemony, N6 china_alternative_order]`

**competing_frames**:
- N2: tech decoupling is necessary; chip exports to China are a dual-use risk; export controls and friendshoring are proportionate to systemic rivalry.
- N5: chip war is hegemonic containment; export controls are economic warfare and collective punishment; multipolar tech alternatives are corrective.
- N6: indigenous capability is a development-first sovereign right; bloc-mentality controls are themselves the destabiliser; win-win cooperation is the alternative.
- *Missing narrative gap*: no v2 narrative captures the EU sovereign-tech / digital-sovereignty position (Chips Act EU, GDPR-as-export, AI Act extraterritoriality, gigafactory subsidies). See closing section.

## FN6. `bab_el_mandeb_red_sea_passage`

**Name**: Bab el-Mandeb and Red Sea shipping passage

**Description**: A 30-km strait carrying roughly 12% of global trade and a substantial share of European energy. The contested phenomenon is who polices it, on what authority, against whom. Houthi attacks, Operation Prosperity Guardian, Aspides EU mission, Iranian-flagged vessel transits, US-UK strikes, Egyptian Suez-revenue collapse, Chinese PLA-N base in Djibouti, regional basing concentration. Distinct from FN2 (Iran nuclear) because the contest is over *maritime governance under attack*, not enrichment. Persistent: the chokepoint geography does not move.

**centroid_ids**: `[AFRICA-HORN, MIDEAST-IRAN, AMERICAS-USA, NON-STATE-EU, NON-STATE-NATO, MIDEAST-SAUDI, ASIA-CHINA]`

**topic_keywords**: `Bab el-Mandeb`, `Red Sea`, `Houthi shipping`, `Suez`, `Aspides`, `Prosperity Guardian`, `Djibouti`, `Yemen strikes`, `freedom of navigation Red Sea`

**competing_narratives**: `[N3 west_iran_proxy_threat, N5 multipolar_anti_us_hegemony, N21 gulf_regional_de_escalation, N24 africa_great_power_competition_navigation]`

**competing_frames**:
- N3 (proxy-network slice): Houthis are Iranian proxies; strikes are legitimate counterterrorism and freedom-of-navigation enforcement.
- N5: Houthi attacks are legitimate resistance to Israeli operations in Gaza; US-UK strikes are foreign aggression on a sovereign state.
- N21: Gulf prefers de-escalation via dialogue with Tehran over kinetic confrontation; hedging multipolar position.
- N24: African coastal states navigate competing presences; the chokepoint is a sovereignty asset, not a foreign-policed channel.

## FN7. `south_china_sea_features`

**Name**: South China Sea contested features

**Description**: Specific reefs, shoals, and EEZ overlaps — Scarborough, Second Thomas Shoal (Ayungin), Spratly features, Paracels — and the legal regime governing them. Distinct from FN1 (Taiwan) because the dispute is over *maritime features and EEZs* with multiple ASEAN claimants, not a 23-million-person polity. Distinct from FN5 (chips) because it's territorial-maritime, not technological. Persistent: reefs don't move; the 2016 PCA ruling is permanent text; the contest is over compliance.

**centroid_ids**: `[ASIA-CHINA, ASIA-SOUTHEAST, AMERICAS-USA, ASIA-JAPAN, OCEANIA-AUSTRALIA]`

**topic_keywords**: `South China Sea`, `Scarborough`, `Second Thomas Shoal`, `Ayungin`, `Spratly`, `Paracels`, `nine-dash line`, `UNCLOS`, `Permanent Court ruling`, `reef militarisation`, `coast guard SCS`

**competing_narratives**: `[N20 asean_south_china_sea_sovereignty, N2 west_china_strategic_competition, N5 multipolar_anti_us_hegemony, N6 china_alternative_order]`

**competing_frames**:
- N20: nine-dash line is illegitimate per the 2016 PCA ruling; reef militarisation and coast-guard ramming are aggression; transparency strategy and UNCLOS adherence are the answer.
- N2: SCS is the front line of the China challenge; freedom-of-navigation operations and Indo-Pacific alliances enforce the rules-based maritime order.
- N5: FONOPS are hegemonic provocation; ASEAN should resolve regionally without US interference.
- N6: indivisible security and bilateral dialogue are the path; bloc-mentality FONOPS escalate.

## FN8. `korean_peninsula_security`

**Name**: Korean peninsula security regime

The contested phenomenon is the regime governing the peninsula — extended deterrence, sanctions enforcement, exercise tempo, the very legitimacy of US presence. Persistent on a multi-generational horizon. Distinct from FN1/FN7 because the actor structure (DPRK, ROK, US, China, Russia) and the pivotal facts (warhead miniaturisation, Hwasong launches) are unique.

**centroid_ids**: `[ASIA-NORKOREA, ASIA-SOUTHKOREA, AMERICAS-USA, ASIA-CHINA, EUROPE-RUSSIA, ASIA-JAPAN]`

**topic_keywords**: `North Korea`, `DPRK`, `Hwasong`, `ICBM`, `USFK`, `KCNA`, `nuclear test`, `armistice`, `Camp David trilateral`, `Kim Jong-un`, `Korea peninsula`

**competing_narratives**: `[N17 korea_peninsula_existential_security, N18 dprk_nuclear_survival, N5 multipolar_anti_us_hegemony, N19 japan_defence_normalisation]`

**competing_frames**:
- N17: DPRK is an existential threat; Kill Chain, KAMD, KMPR, extended deterrence and the trilateral framework are the answer.
- N18: US troops are a foreign occupation force; nuclear weapons are the only credible deterrent against regime change; alignment with Russia and China is survival.
- N5: US presence is hegemonic; sanctions are collective punishment; resolution requires withdrawal of US forces.

## FN9. `israel_palestine_status`

**Name**: Israel-Palestine status

**Description**: The status of Gaza, the West Bank, East Jerusalem, settlements, refugees, and the right of return — and the legitimacy of force used by each side. Distinct from FN2 (Iran nuclear) and FN6 (Red Sea) even though the same actors recur, because the contested phenomenon is *this specific land and population*, not enrichment or shipping. Persistent across decades, every administration, every ceasefire, every reconstruction plan. The April "Iran War" zone model conflated this with FN2 and FN6 — a precision loss the FN model corrects.

**centroid_ids**: `[MIDEAST-ISRAEL, AMERICAS-USA, MIDEAST-IRAN, MIDEAST-SAUDI, NON-STATE-EU]`

**topic_keywords**: `Gaza`, `West Bank`, `East Jerusalem`, `settlements`, `Hamas`, `IDF Gaza`, `two-state`, `ICJ Israel`, `ICC arrest warrant`, `Rafah`, `Hostages`, `UNRWA`

**competing_narratives**: `[N3 west_iran_proxy_threat, N5 multipolar_anti_us_hegemony, N21 gulf_regional_de_escalation]`

**competing_frames**:

## FN12. `greenland_arctic_acquisition`

**Name**: Greenland and territorial-acquisition discourse

**Description**: A specific FN nested under the broader Arctic FN (FN4) but distinct enough to flag separately because the contested phenomenon is *acquisition*, not *governance regime*. US discourse on acquiring Greenland (and re-asserting Panama Canal control, pressuring Canada on union) breaks a 70-year norm against territorial transfer in the Western Hemisphere. Distinct because most FNs contest *current* ownership; this one contests the *transfer norm itself*. Persistent if the discourse persists past one administration.

**centroid_ids**: `[AMERICAS-USA, AMERICAS-CANADA, EUROPE-NORDIC, NON-STATE-EU]`

**topic_keywords**: `Greenland`, `acquire Greenland`, `Panama Canal`, `Denmark Greenland`, `Nuuk`, `Arctic acquisition`, `51st state`, `union Canada`

**competing_narratives**: `[N7 us_hemispheric_primacy, N14 arctic_coastal_sovereignty, N12 eu_ukraine_enlargement]`

**competing_frames**:
- N7: Greenland acquisition and Panama reclamation are hemispheric-primacy logic for an era of great-power competition; foreign powers must be excluded from the approaches.
- N14: Greenland is Danish/Greenlandic sovereign territory; Arctic governance is the prerogative of the eight coastal states, not an extra-regional power.
- N12 (open-door / European-values slice): EU values-based order rejects coercive territorial transfer; the discourse undermines the post-1945 norm against acquisition by pressure.

*Reflection*: borderline whether to keep this as a separate FN from FN4 (Arctic governance). Argument for separation: the *transfer norm* is a distinct contested phenomenon from *Arctic regime governance*; keeping them merged hides this. Argument against: same actors, overlapping centroids, risk of dilution. Recommend keeping separate in v0 to expose the missing-narrative gap on the post-1945 acquisition norm.

## Missing narratives surfaced by this seed list

The v2 worksheet does not cover the following positions, and ≥1 seed FN above needs them to be complete. These are flagged here, then expanded in `narrative_audit_v2.md` Section C.

1. **EU digital / tech sovereignty** — needed for FN5 (AI/semiconductors). The EU has a distinct frame: Chips Act EU, GDPR-as-export-norm, AI Act extraterritoriality, gigafactory and cloud-sovereignty programs. Currently invisible in v2 — only N2 (Western tech decoupling, US-led) and N6 (Chinese alternative order) cover the chip contest, leaving Europe's third position absent.

2. **EU green-deal / CBAM-as-climate-leadership** — needed for FN10 (climate regulation regime). The EU climate-leadership frame (CBAM as legitimate carbon-border policy, ETS as global model, Green Deal as competitiveness strategy) has no v2 narrative.

3. **US energy security and dominance** — needed for FN10. The US frame on LNG export expansion, fossil-fuel-subsidy-language resistance, Paris Agreement reversibility has no v2 narrative; it sits awkwardly between N7 (hemispheric primacy) and absent.

4. **Global South climate-finance-justice** — needed for FN10. The loss-and-damage / climate-debt / transition-minerals-revenue frame from African, South Asian, and Latin American states is a self-frame distinct from N5's general anti-hegemony framing. It might or might not deserve its own narrative; flag for discussion.

5. **Palestinian self-framing** — needed for FN9. v2 currently frames the Israel-Palestine contest entirely through external coalitions (Israeli, US, Iran-aligned, Gulf, EU). Palestinian Authority, Hamas-as-self-described-resistance, and broader Palestinian-civil-society framings are absent as a coalition. Borderline — Palestinian framing partly subsumed under N5 — but worth flagging.

6. **Post-1945 territorial-transfer norm** — possibly needed for FN12. A Western-coalition narrative explicitly defending the post-1945 norm against territorial acquisition by pressure (distinct from N1's Russia-focused threat-response and N12-eu_ukraine_enlargement's enlargement framing). Borderline — could be folded into existing narratives — flag for discussion.

## Reflections on the seed list

**FNs that the April "5 cross-country zones" model would have surfaced**: FN3 (Ukraine territory), FN9 (Israel-Palestine), FN6 (Bab el-Mandeb) — all hot, multi-country. Probably FN1 (Taiwan) given the chip-Taiwan-cross-country activity heat.

**FNs the April model would have missed**: FN4 (Arctic — slow, geographic), FN5 (AI/semiconductors — regulatory/technological, low kinetic heat), FN10 (climate regulation — pure normative regime), FN11 (electoral legitimacy in the Americas — slow, regime-specific), FN12 (Greenland acquisition — narrow, single-country focus). Five of twelve. This is the core argument for the FN model over the April zone model: it surfaces structurally contested phenomena that have no kinetic-heat signature.

**Seed list is heavy on Asia-Pacific** (FN1, FN5, FN7, FN8) and **light on Africa proper** (FN6 is Horn-adjacent; FN10 includes African centroids but isn't African-led). This reflects where the v2 narrative coverage is strong, not where the world's friction is. Future passes should consider Sahel governance (military-junta vs ECOWAS legitimacy), DRC-Rwanda mineral conflict (currently only N26), and Ethiopia-Eritrea-Tigray as candidates if narrative coverage extends.

**FN12 (Greenland acquisition)** is the most uncertain inclusion — defensible on the transfer-norm grounds, but borderline whether it deserves its own FN vs nesting under FN4. User call.
