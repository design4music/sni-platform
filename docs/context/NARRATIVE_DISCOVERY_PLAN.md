# Narrative Discovery & Evolution Plan

**Date drafted**: 2026-05-13
**Status**: Planning. No implementation yet. For review after holiday.
**Linked**: Asana ticket (TBD — to be appended by user).

## Why this doc exists

The friction-node architecture as shipped is **top-down**: curators draft
narratives (claim + stance + publisher cohort + framing_keywords), then
the system fills them with data via `publisher × centroid × fn_anchor`
conjunction. This works well for stable contested phenomena. It does
NOT automatically catch:

- Emergent narrative framings the curator hasn't anticipated.
- Publisher cohort drift (editorial line of a publisher changing over
  months).
- New contested phenomena (new atomic FNs) emerging from real-world
  events.
- Cross-FN meta-patterns (Erdogan's multi-vector diplomacy is visible
  only across mediator + Iran-spillover + a missing Russia FN, not
  inside any one FN).

Politics is a living thing. Turkey is the canonical example raised
2026-05-13: in recent years it has hosted Erdogan's multi-vector play
(US-Russia-Ukraine-Israel-Iran-Caucasus-EU), volume swings between
3k titles/180d and the potential for 30k+ if a major event lands, and
its contested surfaces transform faster than a curator can keep up
without help.

This doc captures what the architecture catches automatically today,
what it doesn't, and three plausible directions for evolution. **No
decision yet.**

## What auto-evolves today

Without any curator intervention, the system already reflects:

- **Volume dynamics**: counts on every brick (narrative title row) and
  every activity-chart sparkline update with each ingest cycle. A
  10× surge in Turkey volume is visible within hours.
- **Publisher-cohort behavior at the volume layer**: if a publisher
  in cohort starts publishing 10× more on a topic, the cohort's
  share of the narrative's attribution grows automatically.
- **Anchor-matched cross-centroid flow**: titles that mention an FN's
  anchor + come from a cohort publisher + overlap geographic scope
  attribute to the narrative regardless of CTM.

## What does NOT auto-evolve

- **The narrative set itself** is curated. A new contested phenomenon
  (e.g., "Turkey-Russia rapprochement" or "Aegean-East-Med flare-up")
  is invisible until a human drafts the FN + narratives.
- **Publisher cohort composition** is a hand-drafted snapshot. If a
  publisher's editorial line shifts (e.g., Le Monde from "engage" to
  "treat as adversary" on Turkey), no automatic detection.
- **fn_anchor vocabulary** is hand-drafted (or deepseek-extracted from
  a corpus snapshot). New place names, new operations, new entities
  enter the corpus and bypass the gate until added.
- **framing_keywords** are hand-drafted per narrative. Stale or
  thin lists silently degrade the loaded-vocabulary tier ranking
  (D-08x, 2026-05-13).
- **Cross-FN meta-narratives**: the architecture has no first-class
  representation of patterns that span multiple FNs.

## Three layers of evolution work

Each layer is independently useful. They stack rather than replace.

### Layer 1 — Curation maintenance habit (cheapest)

Treat narratives + cohorts + anchors as **versioned editorial code**.
Adopt a monthly cadence:

- For each theater: re-audit `friction_nodes.centroid_ids` against
  the actual CTM distribution of attributed events; widen if
  legitimate centroids are missing.
- For each narrative: re-audit `publishers` cohort; remove publishers
  that no longer match the framing, add new ones whose editorial line
  has shifted.
- For each FN: re-run `extract_fn_anchor_via_deepseek.py` against a
  fresh corpus slice; diff against current anchor; promote new
  high-precision tokens.
- For each narrative: re-audit `framing_keywords` against a sample
  of attributed titles; replace stale phrases.

Output: a single curator's checklist. No code. No new tables. Just
discipline + a doc + a cron-reminder.

**Cost**: 2-4 hours/month per analyst. Catches drift but not
emergent narratives.

### Layer 2 — Discovery prompts (light addition, high leverage)

A weekly "narrative health" digest, generated by a small SQL +
optional LLM job. For every active FN, surface to the curator:

1. **Unframed corpus growth**: titles matching the FN anchor + centroid
   but attributed to NO narrative (because no publisher cohort fit).
   "Turkey FN: 142 new titles this week unattached to any narrative;
   top publishers in this unattached set are: X, Y, Z."
2. **Cohort drift**: publishers currently in narrative N's cohort
   whose share of Turkey-coverage has changed >2σ from baseline.
   Direction: covering more, or less. Doesn't auto-edit; flags.
3. **Sub-cluster candidates**: inside an atomic FN, run a lightweight
   signal-co-occurrence clustering (Phase 4 already does this for
   events) on the FN's titles; if a sub-cluster has >50 titles and
   a distinct publisher cohort from the existing narratives, flag
   as "candidate sub-narrative".
4. **Stance language drift**: titles in a narrative whose body
   uses framing language NOT in `framing_keywords`. Top 10 candidate
   keywords per narrative per week.
5. **Cross-FN co-occurrence**: titles attributed to two FNs on the
   same theater. If two FNs co-occur in >X% of attributions, flag as
   a potential meta-narrative.

Output: a markdown digest (or a Slack message). Curator reads,
decides what to act on. Doesn't auto-create narratives.

**Cost**: 2-4 weeks of build. Catches drift AND most emergent
narratives at the cost of a curator reading the digest weekly.

### Layer 3 — Bottom-up narrative discovery (architectural)

Reverse the polarity. Mine emergent narrative clusters from publisher
correlations + signal co-occurrence; use an LLM-as-judge layer to
label and propose narratives; human validates.

This is essentially what `extract_stance_narratives.py` attempted
before D-071 retired it. The retired approach had a 96% orphan rate.
A new bottom-up attempt would have to learn from those failures:

- Cluster on (publisher_correlation, signal_correlation, time_window)
  not on title-text alone.
- Use the existing FN anchor + centroid as scope, not start from raw
  corpus.
- LLM proposes a narrative claim + stance from the cluster's titles;
  human approves before the narrative is created.

**Cost**: 2-3 months of build. Promises emergent-narrative detection
but high risk of noise. Should only be attempted after Layer 2
proves the discovery-prompt approach is insufficient.

## Sleeping FNs / placeholder narratives (user's contribution)

A complement to all three layers. The corpus has 4 months of data
(2026 only). Many real-world contested phenomena are absent from this
window — Turkey-Russia frenemy dynamic, Turkey-Greece East Med,
Turkey-Caucasus, US-Cuba relations, Falklands, Western Sahara, etc.
We can pre-curate FNs for these **without** waiting for volume.

### Proposed model

A friction_node has a new column `friction_nodes.activity_state`:

- `live` — current default, sufficient volume to display fully
- `sleeping` — curated narrative set exists, but title/event volume
  below a display threshold (e.g., <50 titles in last 180 days)
- `archived` — historically significant but currently inert
  (Falklands War on a normal day)

A `sleeping` FN renders in a **mini-UI mode**:

- The page exists at `/friction-nodes/<slug>` (for SEO and direct
  linking)
- Shows the editorial summary (curator's prose) prominently — this
  is what tells the reader the contested surface
- Shows the narratives + claim text + stance brick at zero or near-zero
  width — the contest is *theoretical* until news appears
- Activity chart shows a flat zero with a label "Inert in this period"
- Sample titles section shows "No titles in current window" or, if
  there are a handful, displays them as-is
- A sleeping FN can wake into `live` automatically when volume crosses
  the threshold

This lets us pre-build the map of "all contested phenomena we know
about" without having to wait for a Falklands flare-up before
drafting the FN. The architecture becomes a **theory of contested
politics**, not a reactive cataloguer.

### Benefits

- The map of friction nodes becomes editorially complete (good for
  positioning the product as a strategic intelligence map vs. a news
  aggregator).
- New events on a Falklands or US-Cuba flare-up don't need narrative
  curation in a hurry — the FN already exists, waking up is
  automatic.
- Provides a natural slot for sleeping FNs the curator adds based on
  Layer 2 discovery prompts.

### Open questions

- Sleeping threshold: <50 events? <20 titles? Per-180-days or
  per-30-days?
- Promotion criteria: auto-wake, or curator-promoted?
- Should `archived` differ from `sleeping` operationally?
- Sleeping FN narratives without any attributed titles need a placeholder
  display — what does an empty narrative card look like?
- Sleeping FNs in sitemap — index or noindex? (Lean noindex until
  awake.)
- How does the FN sitemap and the "Conflicts" navigation present
  sleeping FNs vs live ones?

## Specific Turkey FN gaps surfaced 2026-05-13

The current Turkey theater has 4 atomic FNs (mediator role, democratic
backsliding, Kurdish question, Iran-war spillover). Real contested
surfaces NOT covered:

1. **Turkey-Russia frenemy dynamic** — S-400 deal, energy
   dependence, Wagner co-presence in Syria/Libya, Erdogan-Putin
   call against Iran strike, Tartus/Khmeimim base future
   pass-through. Real volume in our corpus though dispersed across
   other FNs.
2. **Turkey-Greece / Aegean / East Med** — Cyprus, EEZ disputes,
   hydrocarbon licences, eKathimerini cohort active.
3. **Turkey in Caucasus** — Azerbaijan post-2020 war, Armenia
   normalisation attempts, Iran-Azerbaijan tension proxy via
   Turkey. Sparse but identifiable.
4. **Turkey-Ukraine** — Bayraktar drones, grain corridor, Erdogan
   as broker (overlaps mediator role but worth separating).
5. **Turkey economic / lira crisis** — Erdogan unorthodoxy on
   rates, foreign-currency reserves drama. Mostly financial-page
   coverage that doesn't carry geopolitical stance.

Of these, the first three are good candidates for sleeping or partial
FNs we draft and seed. The Ukraine and economy items are weaker —
arguably already inside mediator_role or out of theater scope.

## Recommended next-six-months direction (for review)

1. **Layer 1 maintenance habit**: monthly curation review. Cheap.
   Start immediately as a recurring event.
2. **Sleeping FNs architecture**: ~1-2 weeks of work to add
   `activity_state` column, mini-UI mode, and wake-up logic.
   Curator value is high.
3. **Layer 2 discovery prompts** (selected first items only):
   weekly unframed-corpus + cohort-drift + cross-FN-cooccurrence
   digest. Start with stats, add LLM-as-judge sub-cluster labelling
   later. 2-4 weeks.
4. **Layer 3 bottom-up discovery**: parked. Revisit only if Layer 2
   proves insufficient.

This direction keeps the architecture top-down (intentional curation
of contested surfaces) while adding observability and automation
around what to curate next. It does not commit us to the bottom-up
narrative-mining experiment that failed once (D-071).

## Open decisions to take

1. Adopt Layer 1 maintenance habit as a recurring item — yes/no.
2. Build Sleeping FNs architecture — when (timing, sprint scope).
3. Layer 2 discovery prompts scope — which signals first, what

4. Pre-curate the missing Turkey FNs (Russia frenemy, Greece East
