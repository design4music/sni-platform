# FN Map: Strategic Asset Layer — Data Sources & Precision

Status: prototype on `fn-map` branch (2026-07-03). This document records
where every class of map data comes from, how precise it is, and what its
license requires. It is the internal seed for the future public
"Sources & Methods" page.

## Architecture in one paragraph

The map has two first-class object types. **Conflicts** (friction-node
theaters, red warning badges) are the dynamic layer, driven by the news
pipeline's event counts. **Strategic assets** (category-colored dots and
lines) are the static layer: chokepoints, ports, production clusters,
facilities, trade corridors, pipelines. Conflicts press on assets
(`friction_nodes.affected_asset_ids`); routes additionally inherit
pressure from any chokepoint they transit (`meta.via_asset_ids`).
Selecting a conflict lights up participating countries, draws spokes to
their capitals, and reddens every pressed asset and route.

## Data classes, sources, precision

### 1. Conflict anchors & participants
- **Source:** hand-curated (this repo, `20260703_fn_anchor_points.sql`,
  `20260703_centroid_map_points.sql`).
- **What:** one epicenter point per regional theater (Gaza, Donbas front,
  Kashmir LoC...); one capital/representative point per centroid.
- **Precision:** editorial judgment; epicenters are symbolic, not
  operational front lines. Capitals are exact.
- **Confidence:** high for capitals; epicenters are a modeling choice and
  documented as such.

### 2. Strategic assets (points & polygons)
- **Source:** hand-curated in three batches
  (`20260703_seed_strategic_assets.sql`, `..._batch2.sql`, `..._batch3.sql`),
  drafted by LLM research against public knowledge (USGS/EIA/port
  authorities), reviewed in-repo.
- **What:** 9 chokepoints, 58 ports, 28 production clusters, 6 facilities.
- **Precision:** ports are point-accurate to the harbor; production
  clusters are coarse 4-6 vertex hulls around the region (Pilbara,
  Katanga, corn belt) — deliberately schematic.
- **Rendering note:** co-located assets are spread on a ~0.5 deg ring so
  dots never stack; a dot's on-screen position is approximate by design.
- **Criticality (1-5):** hand-assigned. 5 = systemic single point of
  failure (Hormuz, Malacca, Suez, Taiwan Strait, TSMC, Ras Laffan),
  rendered with a double ring.

### 3. Trade corridors (14 lanes)
- **Source:** generated by `scripts/generate_trade_corridors.py` using
  **searoute** (MIT license, Eurostat), which routes over the Oak Ridge
  National Labs Global Shipping Lane Network enriched with AIS data.
- **What:** major seaborne lanes between our seeded ports (Asia-Europe,
  Transpacific, Gulf-Asia crude, Pilbara-China iron ore...).
- **Precision:** follows real shipping-lane geometry; water-only by
  construction. Simplified to <= ~150 points per lane.
- **Bonus data:** searoute reports the maritime passages each lane
  transits; these are stored as `meta.via_asset_ids` (mapped to our
  chokepoint assets) and power route-level pressure propagation. This is
  the embryo of the future `asset_flows` dependency layer.
- **Confidence:** high for geometry; lane SELECTION (which 14 lanes) is
  editorial.

### 4. Pipelines (6 majors)
- **Source (in progress):** Global Energy Monitor's Global Oil/Gas
  Infrastructure Trackers, per-project route GeoJSON from the public
  `GlobalEnergyMonitor/GOIT-GGIT-pipeline-routes` repository.
  **License: CC BY 4.0 — public page must credit Global Energy Monitor.**
- **Status:** the original hand-drawn routes were judged untrustworthy and
  deactivated (`20260703_deactivate_linear_assets.sql`). They return only
  with GEM's researched geometry (`20260703_gem_pipeline_routes.sql`),
  simplified to map precision. A pipeline whose GEM route cannot be
  confidently located stays off the map.
- **Principle:** a wrong line is worse than no line.

### 5. Event pressure (stress, intensity, dormancy)
- **Source:** WorldBrief's own pipeline — `event_friction_nodes` counts
  aggregated per theater, sqrt-normalized; dormant = no events for 90 days.
- **Precision:** as good as the news pipeline's FN matching (keyword +
  centroid + primary_target filters; see FN bootstrap docs).
- **Freshness ceiling:** ingestion runs every 12h; the map's `revalidate`
  is 3600s but underlying data moves at most twice a day.

## Known limitations (candidates for the public confidence statement)

1. Asset list is curated, not exhaustive — absence of an asset means
   nothing.
2. Conflict epicenters are symbolic points, not front lines.
3. Route pressure is topological (transits a pressed chokepoint), not a
   measured traffic disruption.
4. Production-cluster polygons are indicative hulls, not concession
   boundaries.
5. Event counts measure media attention, not casualty/severity ground
   truth.
6. Dot positions near dense clusters are nudged for legibility.
