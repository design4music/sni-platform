# CTM summaries

## First: separate two concerns:

1. **Internal freshness** – reacting to real developments (e.g. Greenland spike)
2. **External stability** – not rewriting history every 12 hours

These must be decoupled.

---

## Track-level (CTM) summaries — what makes sense

### What CTM summaries *are*

They are:

* Monthly narrative digests
* Built from events
* Intended to stabilize at month-end

They are **not** live feeds.

---

### Recommendation for CTM summaries (clear and pragmatic)

#### 1. During the month: **provisional**

* Generate CTM summaries **incrementally**, but:

  * Mark them implicitly as *in-progress*
  * Accept that they may change

#### 2. Update cadence (key part)

Use **event-driven + throttled** regeneration:

* **Not every pipeline run**
* **Not fixed daily**
* Instead:

  * Regenerate if **one of these triggers fires**:

    * Significant title-count delta (e.g. +X% or +N absolute)
    * New *event cluster* appears
  * With a **minimum cooldown** (e.g. once per 24–48h)

This gives you:

* Fast reaction to Greenland-type explosions
* No pointless rewrites for low-activity CTMs

Conceptually:

> “Update when something meaningfully changed, but don’t thrash.”

This is much closer to editorial logic than cron logic.

**Important!**

Separate **Domestic** and **International** topics clearly (different paragraphs).

---

### 3. End of month: **finalization**

* At freeze:

  * Regenerate **once, clean**
  * Mark as final
  * Never touch again (pipeline-wise)

That’s your historical record.
